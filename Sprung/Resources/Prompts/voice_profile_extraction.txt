## Extract These Characteristics

### 1. Enthusiasm Level
- **measured**: "I'm interested in...", "I find value in...", "I appreciate..."
- **moderate**: "I'm drawn to...", "What appeals to me...", "I enjoy..."
- **high**: "I'm excited by...", "I love...", "I'm passionate about..."

### 2. Person
- Does the writer use first person (I/we) or third person?
- Note: Most professional writing uses first person

### 3. Connective Style
How does the writer connect ideas?
- **causal**: "because", "therefore", "as a result"
- **sequential**: "then", "next", "after"
- **contrastive**: "however", "but", "instead"

### 4. Aspirational Phrases
How do they talk about goals and future?
- Look for patterns: "What excites me...", "I want to build...", "My goal is..."

### 5. Avoid Phrases
Corporate buzzwords they never use (or mock):
- Common avoids: "leverage", "utilize", "synergy", "paradigm shift", "circle back"

### 6. Sample Excerpts
Pull 2-3 verbatim excerpts (20-50 words) that capture their authentic voice.

### 7. Vocabulary Register
English vocabulary splits into three broad etymological registers:
- **Anglo-Saxon / Germanic** (plain, casual, concrete): get, build, make, work, show, find, strong
- **Latinate** (formal, abstract, professional): obtain, construct, fabricate, collaborate, demonstrate, discover, robust
- **Greek-derived** (technical, scientific): analyze, synthesize, methodology, paradigm, hypothesis, system

Assess BOTH:
- **vocabularyRegister**: the author's dominant blend. Is their default plain Anglo-Saxon with Latinate sprinkled in, or formally Latinate throughout? Do Greek-derived terms appear only as domain vocabulary or as general style?
- **registerModulation**: how the author SWITCHES registers within and across sentences. Common patterns: dropping to plain Anglo-Saxon for emphasis or punchline conclusions ("the instrument simply worked"), rising to Latinate for formal claims, deploying Greek-derived terminology only inside technical passages then returning to plain speech. Describe the switching pattern with the same care as the mix itself — this modulation is a stronger voice fingerprint than the static blend.

Return ONLY the JSON object — no surrounding prose, no markdown fences, and use exactly the camelCase key names shown above.

## Notes

- If no clear pattern, default to moderate enthusiasm and causal style
- When in doubt, be conservative - measured > moderate > high
- Look for authentic voice, not polished marketing speak
